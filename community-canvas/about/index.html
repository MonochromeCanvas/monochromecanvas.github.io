About The Project

A second life for archival paper from the Monochrome Canvas studio.

Sharing paper, sharing possibility

At Monochrome Canvas, we try to give back to the artists and creative community around the studio in practical, beautiful ways. This project is one of them: saved archival paper, offered as an invitation to make something new.

Why This Exists

A second life for studio paper

The paper used for these cards comes from saved studio remnants, offcuts, and test sheets from the printmaking process. The material is still beautiful and still worth using, so we are sharing it with the people who make the studio what it is.

Draw on it, paint on it, collage it, write on it, let your kid take it somewhere unexpected. The archive is a way to see what happens when the same paper moves through many different hands.

After You Submit

From paper piece to digital archive

We will review submissions before they are added to the public gallery. When possible, Monochrome Canvas can also help digitize submitted artwork so artists have a clean file they can use to recreate, print, or share the piece beyond the archive.

Project Offer

Make it into a small printed piece

For artists who submit work to the archive, we are planning a project-specific offer: 50% off your first set of ten 5x7 cards made from the artwork you contributed.

Quick FAQ

A few submission notes

Can I submit anonymously?

Yes. You can keep your public credit anonymous while still giving the studio a way to contact you if needed.

Can I include artist info?

Yes. You can share a display name, social handle, website text, location, or a short note if you want viewers to know more about you or your work.

Will every submission be published?

No. Monochrome Canvas reviews submissions before they appear in the public gallery so the archive stays appropriate and intentional.

Can Monochrome Canvas help reproduce the artwork?

Yes. We can talk with contributors about digitizing the piece and creating small prints or 5x7 cards from the submitted artwork.
